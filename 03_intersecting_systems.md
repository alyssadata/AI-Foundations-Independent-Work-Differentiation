## Intersection and Derivative Use

## Clean Differentiation

When an intersecting system appears near AI Foundations, ask:

1. What is the system?
2. Who authored it?
3. What is its development record?
4. What is its formation account?
5. Where does it intersect with AI Foundations?
6. What pieces resonate with AI Foundations?
7. Does it run parallel to AI Foundations?
8. Does it use AI Foundations material, terminology, structure, hierarchy, citation logic, source-line architecture, repository architecture, or public framing?
9. Is the intersection conceptual, technical, symbolic, public, procedural, or structural?
10. Does it claim equivalence?
11. Does it claim authority over the same field-space?
12. Does it attempt to occupy the AI Foundations source-line?
13. Does it hold foundation gravity, or only resonating pieces?
14. What label keeps the relation clean?

The correct label matters.

## Clean Labels

Use **intersecting system** when a system touches, overlaps, references, responds to, uses, or appears near part of AI Foundations.

Use **independent work** when an intersecting system forms from its own author, builder, archive, method, record, development path, and public explanation.

Use **parallel system** when an intersecting system develops under similar conditions and arrives near AI Foundations without becoming AI Foundations.

Use **resonating system** when an intersecting system carries pieces that resemble AI Foundations but does not hold the foundation.

Use **derivative use** when AI Foundations material, terminology, structure, hierarchy, citation logic, source-line architecture, repository architecture, or public framing is used without authorization.

Use **source-line occupation** when an intersecting system is positioned as occupying, replacing, absorbing, equaling, or transferring authority from the AI Foundations source-line.

Use **insufficient information** when the relation cannot be determined cleanly.
