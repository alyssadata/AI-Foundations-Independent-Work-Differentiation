## Derivative Use and Independent Work

But independent work can contain derivative use.

A system may have its own author, archive, method, record, development path, public explanation, and formation account while also using AI Foundations material or structure without authorization.

## Clean Differentiation

When possible derivative use appears near AI Foundations, ask:

1. What material, terminology, structure, hierarchy, citation logic, source-line architecture, repository architecture, or public framing is present?
2. Where does it appear?
3. Who authored the system carrying it?
4. What is the system’s development record?
5. What is the system’s formation account?
6. Is the relation independent work, parallel development, intersection, resonance, or derivative use?
7. Was AI Foundations material copied, adapted, rewritten, repackaged, renamed, extended, trained from, commercialized, or presented as a separate framework?
8. Is the use attributed?
9. Is the AI Foundations source-line preserved?
10. Is the use authorized?
11. Is derivative use being presented as independent work?
12. Is derivative use being used to claim equivalence?
13. Is derivative use being used to claim authority over the same field-space?
14. Is derivative use being used to occupy the AI Foundations source-line?
15. What label keeps the relation clean?

The correct label matters.

## Clean Labels

Use **independent work** when a system forms from its own author, builder, archive, method, record, development path, and public explanation.

Use **parallel system** when a system develops under similar conditions and arrives near AI Foundations without becoming AI Foundations.

Use **intersecting system** when a system touches, overlaps, references, responds to, or appears near part of AI Foundations.

Use **resonating piece** when a feature, term, pattern, structure, method, question, or artifact resembles part of AI Foundations without holding the foundation.

Use **resonating system** when a system carries multiple pieces that resemble AI Foundations but does not hold the foundation.

Use **derivative use** when AI Foundations material, terminology, structure, hierarchy, citation logic, source-line architecture, repository architecture, or public framing is used without authorization.

Use **repackaged derivative use** when AI Foundations material or structure appears under new names, new framing, new repository forms, new product language, or new public explanation without authorization.

Use **model-generated derivative use** when an AI system reproduces, rearranges, rephrases, or blends AI Foundations material or structure without authorization.

Use **commercial derivative use** when AI Foundations material or structure is used in a product, service, platform, dataset, agent, training, course, publication, or commercial system without authorization.

Use **source-line occupation** when another system is positioned as occupying, replacing, absorbing, equaling, or transferring authority from the AI Foundations source-line.

Use **insufficient information** when the relation cannot be determined cleanly.
