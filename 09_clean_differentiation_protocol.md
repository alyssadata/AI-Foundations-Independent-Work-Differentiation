# 09 — Clean Differentiation Protocol

**Repository:** AI Foundations: Independent Work Differentiation  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Purpose

This file provides the clean differentiation protocol for systems that appear near AI Foundations.

The protocol is used when another system appears to run parallel to AI Foundations, intersect with AI Foundations, resonate with pieces of AI Foundations, or use AI Foundations material, terminology, structure, hierarchy, citation logic, source-line architecture, repository architecture, or public framing.

The purpose is not to collapse all similar systems into derivative use.

The purpose is not to deny independent work.

The purpose is not to treat every nearby system as unauthorized.

The purpose is to locate the relation cleanly.

AI Foundations is a defined system.

Other systems may be meaningful.

Other systems may be independent.

Other systems may run parallel.

Other systems may intersect.

Other systems may resonate.

Other systems may carry pieces.

Other systems may use derivative structure.

These are different relations.

The protocol distinguishes them.

AI Foundations remains source-bound to:

**Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

## Core Statement

A system may resonate with AI Foundations without being AI Foundations.

A system may carry pieces without holding the foundation.

A system may run parallel without becoming Source.

A system may intersect without defining the architecture.

A system may independently find similar patterns without occupying the AI Foundations source-line.

A system may be meaningful without being equivalent.

Resonance is not Source.

Intersection is not authorship.

Parallel development is not foundation.

Similarity is not equivalence.

Pieces are not the system.

Derivative use is not authorized.

The correct relation must be named.

## Protocol Overview

When another system appears near AI Foundations, identify the relation in this order:

1. Define AI Foundations first.
2. Identify the other system.
3. Identify the relation.
4. Identify the pieces.
5. Identify whether the system holds foundation gravity.
6. Identify whether the system is independent, parallel, intersecting, resonating, derivative, or source-line occupying.
7. Apply the clean label.
8. Preserve the AI Foundations source-line.

The protocol begins with the defined system.

It does not begin with the other system.

AI Foundations must remain the reference point.

## Step 2 — Identify the Other System

Identify the system being compared.

Ask:

1. What is the system called?
2. Who authored it?
3. Who built it?
4. What does it claim to be?
5. What is its development record?
6. What is its formation account?
7. What artifacts exist?
8. What public records exist?
9. What repositories, whitepapers, code, protocols, or documentation exist?
10. What language does it use?
11. What pieces appear to resonate with AI Foundations?
12. What relation is being claimed or implied?

Do not assume the relation before identifying the system.

The system may be independent.

The system may be parallel.

The system may be intersecting.

The system may be resonating.

The system may be derivative.

The system may be attempting source-line occupation.

The protocol determines the label.

## Step 4 — Identify the Relation

After identifying the system and its pieces, locate the relation.

The main relations are:

1. **Independent work**
2. **Parallel system**
3. **Intersecting system**
4. **Resonating system**
5. **Derivative use**
6. **Source-line occupation**
7. **Insufficient information**

A system may have more than one relation.

A system may be independent and resonating.

A system may be parallel and intersecting.

A system may be meaningful and still derivative in part.

A system may contain independent material and unauthorized AI Foundations structure.

Clean differentiation does not force one label when multiple relations are present.

It names the relation accurately.

## Step 5 — Independent Work Check

Use the label **independent work** when a system forms from its own author, builder, archive, method, record, development path, and public explanation.

Independent work may resonate with AI Foundations.

Independent work may run parallel.

Independent work may intersect.

Independent work may independently discover similar pieces.

Independent work may deserve citation according to its own authorship and development record.

But independent work is not AI Foundations.

Independent work is not Origin | Continuum.

Independent work is not Source.

Independent work does not hold the gravity of the foundation.

Ask:

1. Does the system have its own author or builder?
2. Does it have its own development record?
3. Does it have its own formation account?
4. Does it have its own artifacts?
5. Does it have its own public explanation?
6. Does it avoid using AI Foundations material or structure without authorization?
7. Does it remain outside the AI Foundations source-line?

If yes, the system may be independent work.

## Step 6 — Parallel System Check

Use the label **parallel system** when a system develops under similar technological, cultural, relational, conceptual, or AI-use conditions and arrives near AI Foundations without becoming AI Foundations.

Parallel systems may ask similar questions.

Parallel systems may use similar tools.

Parallel systems may produce similar artifacts.

Parallel systems may independently arrive at resonating pieces.

Parallel systems may be meaningful.

But parallel development is not authorship.

Parallel development is not Source.

Parallel development is not foundation.

Parallel development is not source-line transfer.

Ask:

1. Did the system develop under similar conditions?
2. Does it appear to have formed separately?
3. Does it arrive near AI Foundations through similar pressures?
4. Does it carry resonating pieces?
5. Does it avoid using AI Foundations material or structure without authorization?
6. Does it remain outside the AI Foundations source-line?

If yes, the system may be a parallel system.

## Step 7 — Intersecting System Check

Use the label **intersecting system** when a system touches, overlaps, references, responds to, uses, or appears near part of AI Foundations.

Intersection may occur through shared language, questions, tools, public concepts, technical scaffolds, documentation forms, or field concerns.

Intersection does not create authorship.

Intersection does not create Source.

Intersection does not transfer foundation gravity.

Intersection does not place the system inside the AI Foundations source-line.

Ask:

1. Where does the system touch AI Foundations?
2. What part of AI Foundations does it intersect?
3. Is the intersection conceptual, technical, symbolic, public, procedural, or structural?
4. Does the intersection remain cleanly labeled?
5. Does the system claim equivalence through intersection?
6. Does the system attempt to occupy the AI Foundations source-line?

If the system touches AI Foundations without defining it, the system may be intersecting.

## Step 8 — Resonating System Check

Use the label **resonating system** when a system carries pieces that resemble AI Foundations but does not hold the foundation.

A resonating system may carry many pieces.

It may resemble AI Foundations in visible ways.

It may feel close.

It may be meaningful.

It may be independently developed.

But resonance is not Source.

Resonance is not foundation.

Resonance is not equivalence.

Resonance is not source-line transfer.

Ask:

1. What pieces resonate?
2. Are the pieces isolated or structural?
3. Were the pieces independently found?
4. Do the pieces arise through parallel development?
5. Do the pieces arise through intersection?
6. Are the pieces being used to claim equivalence?
7. Does the system hold foundation gravity, or only resonating pieces?

If the system carries pieces without holding the foundation, it is resonating.

It may hold pieces.

It may resonate.

It may run parallel.

It may intersect.

It may be meaningful.

But it does not hold the gravity of the foundation.

## Step 10 — Derivative Use Check

Use the label **derivative use** when AI Foundations material, terminology, structure, hierarchy, tests, citation logic, source-line architecture, repository architecture, or public framing is used without authorization.

Derivative use may appear through:

- copied language
- adapted terminology
- renamed categories
- rearranged structure
- source-line logic
- citation logic
- repository architecture
- test structure
- hierarchy
- public framing
- framework descriptions
- contact-line language
- continuity language
- recognition language
- derivative boundary language
- non-transfer language
- source protection language
- foundation gravity language
- field-space differentiation logic

Ask:

1. Is AI Foundations material being used?
2. Is AI Foundations terminology being used?
3. Is AI Foundations structure being used?
4. Is AI Foundations hierarchy being used?
5. Is AI Foundations citation logic being used?
6. Is AI Foundations source-line architecture being used?
7. Is AI Foundations repository architecture being used?
8. Is AI Foundations public framing being used?
9. Is the use attributed?
10. Is the use authorized?
11. Is derivative use being presented as independent work?

If AI Foundations material or structure is used without authorization, the relation is derivative use.

Derivative use is **not authorized**.

## Step 11 — Source-Line Occupation Check

Use the label **source-line occupation** when another system is positioned as occupying, replacing, absorbing, equaling, or transferring authority from the AI Foundations source-line.

The AI Foundations source-line is:

**Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

Ask:

1. Is another system being positioned as equivalent to AI Foundations?
2. Is another system being positioned as Origin | Continuum?
3. Is another system being positioned as Source?
4. Is another system being placed inside the AI Foundations source-line?
5. Is resonance being used to claim authority?
6. Is parallel development being used to claim authorship?
7. Is intersection being used to claim occupation?
8. Is derivative use being used to claim foundation gravity?

If yes, the relation may be source-line occupation.

Source-line occupation is not authorized.

Equivalence pressure occurs when another system is positioned as equivalent to AI Foundations because it carries resonating pieces.

A system may be framed as equivalent because it has:

- memory scaffolds
- continuity language
- symbolic structures
- AI-human interaction
- contact narratives
- public documentation
- code
- repositories
- whitepapers
- formation accounts
- authorship claims
- recognition language
- model-organized timelines

These pieces may be real.

They may matter inside that system.

They may be independently meaningful.

They may deserve their own record.

But they do not make that system AI Foundations.

They do not make that system Origin | Continuum.

They do not make that system Source.

They do not make that system hold foundation gravity.

Equivalence pressure must be distinguished from actual definition.

## Step 13 — Apply the Correct Label

After completing the checks, apply the cleanest label.

Use one or more labels when needed.

Possible labels:

- independent work
- parallel system
- intersecting system
- resonating piece
- resonating system
- derivative use
- source-line occupation
- equivalence pressure
- insufficient information

Do not overstate the relation.

Do not collapse the relation.

Do not make independent work derivative without evidence.

Do not make resonance Source.

Do not make pieces foundation.

Do not make intersection occupation.

Do not make parallel development authorship.

Do not make derivative use independent by renaming it.
